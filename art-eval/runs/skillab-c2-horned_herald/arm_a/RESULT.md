## Rationale
The card's mechanic is the rally: a 3/3 Beast that, on entering the battlefield,
gives +1/+1 to your whole team. So the art's job is not "a horned beast" but "a
herald whose arrival strengthens the herd." Every generation deliberately stages a
dominant horned herald with an answering herd of smaller beasts — the +1/+1-to-all
made visible as a herd surging at the leader's call. gen_02 wins because it reads
that relationship most unambiguously at 64x32: the large bison-like herald is clearly
the leader, and smaller beasts flank it on BOTH sides, so the eye instantly parses
"one rallying figure + a herd answering" rather than a single muddled mass. Its curved
horns give the cleanest readable silhouette, the composition is balanced, and the
deep-emerald forest keeps it firmly in green's color identity and in the existing
set's warm-creature-against-canopy vocabulary (anchored to wolfbriar_elemental and
surging_beast). gen_05 (most atmospheric, best horns) and gen_04 (best motion) are
strong runners-up if a different read is preferred.
