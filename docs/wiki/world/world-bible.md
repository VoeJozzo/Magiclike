# Magiclike — world bible (living draft)

This folder is the **worldbuilding layer** for [[magiclike]] — the setting's tone, cosmology, color philosophy, and named things. It was seeded from a close reading of the existing card art and the prompt corpus (the world was already *there*; this shard mostly transcribes it), then corrected and extended in design conversation with Joe.

**Status: scratchpad under heavy iteration. Nothing here is locked.** Joe is the taste filter; pages record his authorial notes verbatim where possible and tag everything else by confidence. Challenge anything.

## Status legend

Every claim in this shard carries one of these (explicitly or by section):

- **Authorial** — Joe said it; primary source. Quote, don't paraphrase into something tidier.
- **Observed** — consistently present in the existing art/prompt corpus; cite the cards.
- **Adopted** — a proposal Joe endorsed; working canon until it loses a fight.
- **Parked** — proposed, neither adopted nor killed; see [[names-ledger]].
- **Art-book** — true of how we *make images*, not of the world. See [[art-language]] — this boundary matters and is easy to smear.

## Open questions (the live ones)

1. **How is magic *shown*?** Magic is a verb here — when things happen, so does magic (Way of Kings adjacent). But the setting holds both very low- and very high-fantasy elements, and exactly how that tension resolves is undecided. → [[tone]]
2. ~~How literal is the thread model?~~ **Settled (2026-06-09):** everyone has seen it once, and nobody believes it. → [[threads]]
3. **The war** — one long war or many? Named or never named? (Lean: small windows don't need the war's name.) → [[tone]]
4. **The divine** — unresolved on purpose, and the unresolvedness is canon. Rule refined: depiction allowed, adjudication forbidden — cards may testify; the set never returns a verdict. → [[the-divine]]
5. **Elystra / the player** — is a run one of her lives? Proposed, unreviewed. → [[threads]]
