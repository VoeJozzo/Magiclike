# Meridian — the city that bottles noon *(name liked, page draft)*

The one place in the corpus under full sun. A tiered white-marble city beneath a blazing noon sky, a monumental brass aqueduct on its horizon channeling streams of pale light through twin turbines (**Equatorial Engine**). Classical architecture — fluted columns, bronze statues, an arena with packed stands (**Warrior Champion**, **City of Brass**). In a corpus where light arrives by candle, ember, and dawn, Meridian has noon *on tap* — light as civic infrastructure.

## The theme *(authorial)*

> "It is easy to convince oneself of the superiority of one's nation, one's way of life, when one is untouched by the shaking of the foundation of the world. *Yet.*" — Joe

Meridian is the *yet*. The war ([[tone]]) is somewhere else; the mud is somewhere else; the foundations here are marble and they have never shaken. The city's confidence is genuine, earned, and built directly over the fault line.

## The artificers *(observed)*

Meridian is where the [[threads]] get *handled*:

- **Artifice Triumphant** — a man suspended mid-conversion, half flesh and half brass, five luminous filaments converging at his heart. They are rewearing people.
- **Ingenuity Unbounded** — a palm-sized black cube, seams leaking five colors of light, sitting "perfectly unmarred" in the radial debris of the workshop it just destroyed. They are tying knots they cannot untie.
- **Scrap Hound, Bulwark Automaton, Architect's Codex, Stapler** — the wider craft tradition, from noble clockwork pets to the literal splicing tool.

The corpus's joke and its warning are the same image: the cube is fine. The room is gone.

## For card-makers

Meridian cards want: civic pride rendered in materials (brass, marble, verdigris); ingenuity outpacing wisdom by exactly one workshop at a time; the arena's spectacle as the city's substitute for the war it hasn't met. Meridian is not decadent — decadence knows it's ending. Meridian is *confident*. That's worse.
