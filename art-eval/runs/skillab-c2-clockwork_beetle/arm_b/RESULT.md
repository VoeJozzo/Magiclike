# RESULT — Clockwork Beetle (arm_b)

## BEST: gen_01_seed1211370075.png
`BEST.png` is a byte-identical copy of `gen_01_seed1211370075.png`.

## Why
**Mechanic-enactment.** Clockwork Beetle is a vanilla artifact creature whose only job in art is to read unmistakably as a *mechanical/clockwork automaton built of metal*, not an organic insect — and in metallic neutrals, not a WUBRG color. gen_01 nails this best of the ten:

- The body is clearly **brass/copper plating** with an **oxidized verdigris (teal-patina) carapace** — colorless/artifact identity, zero color-wash.
- The **six jointed metal legs** are individually readable and articulated like a wind-up mechanism, reinforcing "automaton" over "bug."
- It sits in a real **workshop scene** (warm lantern glow upper-left, scattered brass gears on the bench, stone wall behind) — satisfies the "real setting, not a sprite on void" requirement.
- Crucially, it wins on **whole-image gestalt at size**: the head/thorax/legs separate cleanly at 64x32, so the silhouette reads as one coherent mechanical beetle rather than a metallic blob.

## Runner-up
gen_04_seed802200393 — riveted domed carapace, glowing red eye, lantern + bench. Very characterful and arguably the most "machine-eyed," but its shell reads a touch more as a smooth dome than gen_01's exposed-plate body, and gen_01 has the cleaner overall read.

## Batch quality
All 10 succeeded at the core brief (mechanical brass beetle, workshop setting, no organic/color-wash failures). The arm produced a consistent, on-identity spread; the pick was decided on legibility of the whole silhouette.
