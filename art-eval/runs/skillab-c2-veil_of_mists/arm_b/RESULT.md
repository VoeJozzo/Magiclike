## Why (tied to mechanic-enactment)

Veil of Mists is a **bounce** spell — a targeted creature is returned to hand, i.e. *removed from the field by being swallowed and vanishing into the mist*. The art's job is to show that disappearance happening, not a creature standing in fog and not a kill effect.

gen_10 is the only roll where the figure genuinely **reads as vanishing into the veil rather than standing in it**: the armored warrior is half-transparent and desaturated, his upper body breaking up against the pale fog, and his lower body dissolving into a bright white-teal glow at the feet — the body is being *erased into the mist* from the ground up. That is the bounce moment: a creature mid-disappearance, carried off the field. It stays cleanly inside the single-blue identity (cerulean / pale teal / white-grey) over a real twilight-marsh setting, not a sprite on void.

### Why not the others
