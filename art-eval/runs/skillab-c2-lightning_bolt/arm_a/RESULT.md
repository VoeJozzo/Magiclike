## Why this one

Lightning Bolt deals 3 damage to any one target — the mechanic-in-art is *the target
being struck*, not lightning decorating a sky. The chosen direction is a worm's-eye
victim view: a lone figure on a ridge at the instant the bolt lands on him.

gen_04 enacts that most clearly of all six rolls. A vivid pink/electric-red impact
burst sits unmistakably on the figure's upper torso with crimson Lichtenberg arcs
forking off it — the strike is *on him*, not beside him. Red color identity is strong
and saturated (no blue bleed). It reads as a coherent scene at 64x32, not a sprite on
negative space.

It was produced by seed-locking the winning v1 roll (gen_01, same seed) and applying a
one-axis refinement: push the strike point onto the chest and add a white-hot impact
burst. The composition held and the strike-point clarity improved — textbook
seed-locked surgical iteration.

The rejected alternates either rendered the bolt the wrong color (blue/purple) or
beside the figure (decorative, gen_03/gen_05), or flipped the mechanic to the figure
*casting* the lightning (gen_06) — all failures of either color identity or
mechanic-enactment.
