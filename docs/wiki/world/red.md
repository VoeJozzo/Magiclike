# Red — why not make history?

## Philosophy *(authorial)*

Red's deep theme here isn't anger; it's **historical opportunism** — the recognition that the times are large and someone's name is going on them.

> "If we are alive in a time when history is being made… *why not make it*? Some times produce an Alexander or a Genghis Khan or Julius Caesar, so one may as well be them. Someone was going to discover the Americas, so why not Columbus… Someone's nation would do the conquering. Someone's name would be written in the history books. And if cities will be sacked… I'd rather be in the army than an inhabitant. Such are the times." — Joe

That last line is red's whole ethic in seven words: **rather the army than an inhabitant.** It's not sadism and it's not ideology. It's reading the weather of [[tone|the war]] correctly and choosing a side of the city wall while the choosing is good. Red's grandeur and red's atrocity are the same impulse at different scales — the corpus already holds both: Rally the Troops' genuinely beautiful dawn, and Martyred Scholar's red-robed soldiers looting the library ([[blue]]).

## The goblins *(authorial + observed)*

> "Heh, the goblins are just vibing. Living their best lil' goblin lives." — Joe

And they are: war-drummers drumming, warchanters chanting, a raider joyously smashing pots, a duelist dragging a comically oversized sword. But the corpus *also* gives them the set's most tender interiority — **Burnout Shaman** (singed, head in hands at the bar while others party), **Raging Goblin**'s full mental breakdown, **Pyroclasm** consuming a goblin village in lava. The goblins live in the volcano's margins and sometimes the volcano eats the rent.

Hold both registers without forcing them into tragedy-of-the-underclass: the goblins aren't a statement. They're *neighbors*. Comedy and grief in the same cave, never on the same card.

## Open structure question

The corpus shows two red populations: the **legion** (red robes + iron armor — the library's arsonists, organized, ambitious) and the **goblin under-nation** (caves, forges, drums). Whether they're connected — recruitment, vassalage, nothing — is unestablished. Proposed-and-unreviewed: the legion recruits from the caves. Parked name for the legion's campaign: "Cindermarch" — Joe: potential, but smells contrived as a faction name; maybe a *movement* — see [[names-ledger]] for the historians'-coinage reading that might rescue it.

## For card-makers

Red cards want: the moment of seizing (a gate, a torch, a chance); ambition rendered sympathetically from inside the army and honestly from under it; goblins doing goblin things with total commitment. Red's villains don't know they're villains — they think they're *early*.
