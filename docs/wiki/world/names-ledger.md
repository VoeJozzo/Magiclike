# Names ledger — every proper noun, with its status

Joe's instinct, recorded as policy: **not everything needs a name, and "Adjective Nounverber" faction names are a smell.** This page tracks every proposed name so nothing gets canonized by repetition.

## Naming philosophy (proposal, partially endorsed by the instinct above)

Three tiers, and most names never leave tier 1:

1. **Working labels** — internal shorthand for the bible and art direction. Free, cheap, allowed to be generic; they never appear on a card. "The Reclaim" can live here forever and do its job perfectly.
2. **In-world proper nouns** — rare, and *earned by a card needing them*. The bar: would the people in the world plausibly have named this thing? Cities get names (people name cities). Bureaucracies name *themselves* — which is why black's institution can carry a name no other faction needs. Processes, moods, and wars mostly don't get names in a small-windows world ([[tone]]) — a card shows mud and a bell, not "the Third Crusade of the Whatever."
3. **Historians' coinages** — names the world's chroniclers applied *after the fact*. This tier is the rescue for names that smell contrived: propaganda and historiography ARE contrived, in-world. "The Cindermarch" is a bad name for an army and a plausible name for what survivors and chroniclers later called the campaign.

Where I push back (gently, once): factions whose cards will keep recurring need at least a tier-1 handle, or flavor text drifts. But the handle never has to be spoken by anyone who lives there.
