# White — the bypass must go somewhere

## Philosophy *(authorial)*

White is not 'good', though good is often contained within it. Its register, in the hyperspace-bypass frame ([[tone]] §touchstones): **"Unfortunately, the hyperspace bypass needs to go *somewhere*."** Order, mercy, and law — administered by people who no longer get to be innocent about what administering costs.

> "One of the facts about white is that it **dirties easily**. One can be the hopeful, idealistic, (oft-progressive) college student with ideals about the world and the future, and then you… get a job. And then you're the Boring Middle Class, y'know?" — Joe

> "The art on Pacifism isn't a declaration of an ideal — it is, to some extent, the necessary consequence of war." — Joe

## What the corpus shows *(observed)*

- **Righteous Judge** — white at its heaviest: a weary judge, a bloodied sword, a decapitated civilian, four solemn villagers. Justice as a thing someone has to *do*, by hand, in a small square.
- **Pacifism** — a soldier collapsed in a cathedral, bloody sword still in his grip. Not ideal; consequence.
- **Swords to Plowshares** — the verb made literal: blade in the soil, wheat around it. White's best ending, available to almost no one.
- **The angels** — powerful, radiant (Binding Angel atop her pinned demon), and employed entirely in triage and arrest while heaven stays serene above ([[the-divine]]). The divine is a blunt instrument, and man is a fragile thing.
- **Binding Angel chains her demon; she doesn't kill it.** A tendency worth noticing: white *binds* (Pacifism, Encase in Amber's cousin-energy, the arrest) where black *executes* (the Edict). Status: observed pattern, not yet doctrine.
- Dawn is white's hour — loosely ([[art-language]] §3).

## For card-makers

White cards want: the weight of the institution on one person; mercy with a cost ledger attached; the moment idealism meets the job. White's failures aren't hypocrisy-as-villainy — they're *erosion*, one reasonable compromise at a time. A white villain still believes the bypass had to go somewhere.

Parked name: "the Dawnward" (proposed for white's soldiers/clerics/angels collectively — unreviewed; see [[names-ledger]]).
