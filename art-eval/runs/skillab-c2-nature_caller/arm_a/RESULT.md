## Why (tied to mechanic-enactment)

The card's mechanic is "when it enters play, search your library for a creature card and add it to your hand" — the druid **calls a wild ally out of the forest**. The brief's hero is the *act of calling and the beast answering*, not a druid standing in the woods.

gen_06 enacts that more legibly than any other roll in the batch:

- **The calling gesture is unambiguous.** The hooded druid's arm is raised with an open, outstretched hand beckoning toward the stag — a clear *summoning/calling* read at 64×32. Several other rolls (03, 05, 07, 09) lost the druid into foliage or made the gesture unreadable.
- **The beast is visibly *answering*.** The antlered stag has turned its head and angled its body toward the druid — it reads as responding to the call, not just grazing nearby. In gen_01 the druid is absent; in gen_02 the elk dominates and the relationship reads as a wildlife encounter rather than a summons.
- **Both subjects are legible and well separated**, with the gap between them carrying the "call across the clearing" tension — the two-shape silhouette test passes cleanly (hooded caller left, antlered beast right).
- **Color identity is locked GREEN**: moss-green hooded robe, antlers, dark emerald dappled old-growth canopy, mossy trunks. Earthy greens/browns, no off-color contamination.
- **Real forest setting**, not a sprite on void — depth from layered trunks and dappled light, satisfying the no-void requirement.
