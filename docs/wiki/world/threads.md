# Threads — the weave cosmology *(adopted as working model)*

**Everything alive in this world is woven, and a soul is a thread with one loose end.**

Nobody designed this; it was *observed* — the prompt corpus renders life and magic as thread/filament/wisp with total consistency, across every color, over the project's whole life. Joe's verdict: "very cool, very Banner Saga." Adopted as the working cosmology.

## The evidence (observed, across all five colors)

- The soul leaves Soul Reaper's sleeper as a wisp of breath, "like a breath."
- Dread Wraith draws "a thin glowing thread" of life from a dying soldier's mouth.
- Cult Priest's sacrifice rises as "a thin thread of red life" that arcs to where the toll lands.
- Beast Whisperer collects green "knowledge-threads" from animal brows into a leaf-page.
- Hymnwright's quill raises "a pale-cerulean soul-wisp" from the written name.
- Heir to the Burnt House's curse leaves his palm as "a thin filigree thread of bright violet witch-fire."
- Aether Drake protects an ally through a taut luminous tether.
- Artifice Triumphant: a man converted to brass with "five thin luminous filaments… converging around the heart."
- Elystra the Immortal visibly accumulates spiraling threads as she grows (see her art ladder, `reference/html-proto/cards/elystra_the_immortal/README.md`).

## The grammar it gives us (adopted shorthand; phrase freely, don't template)

- A **soul** is a loose thread. Death is the thread pulled free — *visible*, repeatedly, in the art.
- **Necromancy** is re-knotting used thread.
- **Artifacts** are knots that hold without a weaver.
- **A spell** is a knot tied in a moment ([[tone|magic is a verb]] — the tying is the event).

## Where it meets the mechanics

The roguelike meta already *is* this cosmology: [[sticker-system|stickers]] and [[staple-synthesis|staples/splices]] graft and bind — to sticker a card is to splice its weave; the Stapler card literally staples two cards together in a burst of light. **Drafting is gathering threads; a run is a life; losing a run is unraveling; what persists between runs is the knot that held.**

*(Proposal, unreviewed)* Elystra — the only card whose art evolves, who visibly gathers all five threads in her final form — as the player's avatar: each run one of her lives; "the Immortal" meaning *she refuses to stay unwoven*. Keep her origins unstated regardless.

## Open questions

1. ~~How literal is this in-world?~~ **Adopted: *everyone has seen it once, and nobody believes it.*** At a deathbed, in a temple, during a splice — everyone has seen the thread, and the world disagrees about what it was.
   > "Like 'the feeling of being watched' or 'I *know* I wasn't alone in the woods.' Everyone's experienced those sensations, and everyone knows they're very real — and most have the good sense not to believe in them anyway." — Joe
   This keeps the low/high tension ([[tone]] §magic-is-a-verb) as a feature rather than a question to answer: the threads are real, the experience of them is near-universal, and the world's good sense declines the conclusion. (It also rhymes with [[the-divine]]: testimony everywhere, verdict nowhere.)
2. The five hue words used for threads in the art (cerulean / crimson / emerald / ivory / wine-purple) are **art-book palette, not in-world names** — see [[art-language]]. Whether the threads have in-world names at all: open, no urgency.
