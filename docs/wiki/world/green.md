# Green — the Reclaim

## Philosophy *(adopted, with authorial gloss)*

Green is **not a faction — it's a process**. It doesn't fight [[tone|the Long War]]; it composts it. Working name for the process: **the Reclaim** (endorsed as phrasing; whether it's ever an in-world word is open — see [[names-ledger]]).

> "If you are a bear, you simply… don't care about a lot of things. Even the things that *do* affect you are sort of just… how it is." — Joe

And green has a place in the world's succession — the last one in line, and the most patient:

> "Emperors have fallen to kings, kings will fall to warlords, and nature bides its time, waiting for its turn after the warlords." — Joe ([[the-fall]])

Green's indifference rhymes with [[black]]'s but is nothing like it: black *decides* not to care (a judgment, recorded in a ledger); green never held the question. The bear is not callous. The bear is a bear. Tonal touchstone: **"The Snow it Melts the Soonest"** — patient, unhurried, certain; everything melts in its season.

## What the corpus shows *(observed)*

- **Last Druid** — the most beautiful prompt in the log: a dying druid's legs already softening into moss and fallen leaves, a luminous seedling rising from her open hand cradling a fawn-spirit. Death as handoff, not loss. Druids are the Reclaim's hospice workers.
- **Choking Vines** — nature kills a *stag*, one of its own, with documentary violence and "no magical glow." The Reclaim doesn't take sides, including green's.
- **Great Herder** — an elf shepherd guiding ents down a forest path: the closest green gets to organization, and it's *husbandry*, not command.
- Moss is green's signature texture and it's already everywhere in the corpus — on boulders, altars, golems, ruins. Moss is the Reclaim's progress bar.
- The wild things at the edges: Feral Stalker's hidden tiger, Surging Beast's boar, Apex Elder scattering panicked fauna. Green's creatures are not allies; they're *weather with teeth*.

## For card-makers

Green cards want: patience, appetite, succession; the war's leavings being quietly digested (a helm full of soil, saplings in a siege engine); animals that act from animal reasons. Green has no speeches. Its druids don't defend nature — they *attend* it, the way one attends a deathbed or a birth. When green is violent, it's matter-of-fact (Choking Vines), never cruel: cruelty requires the question green never asks.
