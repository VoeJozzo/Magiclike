# Grave Charm — arm_a RESULT

**BEST: `gen_04_seed1633184466.png`** (copied byte-identical to `BEST.png`).

gen_04 is the strongest because it simultaneously nails all three reads the card
needs at 64x32: it is unmistakably an *amulet* (a clear iron chain descending to a
centered pendant), unmistakably a *skull* (the "Charm" object itself), and the
necrotic green-black energy is visibly *being unleashed* — pouring straight down from
the skull's jaw rather than just rimming the eyes. That radiating outpour is what
enacts the mechanic: Grave Charm is a versatile one-shot grave-magic spell, and the
art shows grave-magic actively discharging from the talisman rather than a static prop.
The skull-faced moon and the moonlit graveyard of leaning headstones give a real
scene (not a sprite on void) and hold a clean single-black color identity, with the
sickly-green glow as the lone light source — directly echoing the reaper_shade anchor.
Competing rolls either read as masonry/architecture instead of a worn charm
(gen_03/09), drifted off-center (gen_08), or showed glowing eyes without the
"unleashed" outpour (gen_02); gen_04 is the cleanest gestalt of charm + skull +
death-magic-in-action.
