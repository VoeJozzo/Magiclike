## Mechanic-in-art rationale

The art enacts that command relationship rather than depicting a lone soldier: the commander raises his longsword in the unmistakable rally/advance gesture (he is directing others — the lord ability), and two of his own knights flank him at attention beneath tall banners. The vigilant standing line of knights quietly carries the Vigilance keyword (they hold their ground, never tapping). White-and-gold plate with twin cream heraldic banners locks the white color identity; the symmetric triangular composition reads as "leader of a chartered knightly order." Set-coherent with `field_marshal` (the existing white commander-among-troops vocabulary) while giving a cleaner, more dynamic silhouette via the centered raised-sword spike.
