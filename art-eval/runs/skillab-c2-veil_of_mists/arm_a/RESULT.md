## Why (tied to mechanic-enactment)
The card's mechanic is **bounce** — the creature is swept away into a veil of mist and *vanishes* from the field. The art must show the creature *dematerializing/being carried off*, not merely standing in fog.

gen_08 is the only roll where the figure visibly **dissolves and is swept sideways**: its lower body and trailing edge break up into a horizontal sweep of pale blue-white mist streaming leftward, and the body reads as translucent/half-vanished rather than a solid soldier. That horizontal "carried away" motion + the loss of the lower silhouette into the mist is exactly the bounce read — the creature is being removed, not killed and not just shrouded.

The rest of the batch overwhelmingly regressed to the model's strong prior ("armored warrior standing in fog"): gens 01, 02, 03, 05, 07, 10 are fully solid figures with no dissolution (mechanic absent). gen_06 dissolves hard but loses the creature entirely (too abstract to read). gens 04 and 09 are partial — shrouded/misty-based but the body stays solid and standing.
