# Result — Prodigal Sorcerer (arm_a)

**BEST nominee: `gen_01_seed2081743954.png`**

This frame most clearly enacts the card's mechanic — Tap: deal 1 damage to *any* target — as a *precise ping rather than a blast*. A hooded cerulean-blue wizard, in a dynamic side-on casting pose, sends a single thin blue-white spark arcing from his extended fingertip up to one tiny airborne moth at the top of the frame. The hand-to-target line is unambiguous and the energy reads as a hairline crackle, not a fireball, so the "1 damage to a single target" idea is legible at a glance. It also lands the rest of the brief: correct blue color identity, a frail human-wizard silhouette, and a real scene (stone study, bookshelves, arched window) rather than a sprite on negative space. gen_10 and gen_05 are strong runners-up but read as a more generic "wizard casting" — gen_01 is the one where the *pinging* is the subject.
